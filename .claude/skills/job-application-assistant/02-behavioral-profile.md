# Behavioral Profile

## Overview
Harsh's working pattern is best described as a **high-ownership self-starter**. He does not wait for process to be handed to him; he builds the infrastructure himself (a 1,200+ entry Notion job-search HQ, automated GitHub workflows, personal trackers) and iterates fast toward a working result.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Ownership / initiative | High | Builds his own tools and process rather than waiting to be told what to use |
| Pace preference | High | Prefers fast-paced, startup-like environments with visible momentum |
| Decision style | Situational | Moves quickly on low-stakes calls, slows down and gathers data when the stakes are higher |
| Structure under volume | High | Comfortable managing many parallel threads (applications, outreach, coursework, research) without losing track |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **End-to-end execution:** Consistently takes projects from raw data to a deployed, usable artifact (dashboard, tool, automation) rather than stopping at analysis
- **Systematic output at volume:** Tracks and executes against large numbers of parallel tasks (1,220+ tracked applications, 300+ outreach messages) with structure rather than chaos
- **Written communication:** Strong at concise, specific, non-fluffy outreach and cover letters

## How You Work Best
- High autonomy, clear goals, room to build his own process
- Fast iteration loops (idea to shipped output) rather than long approval chains
- Environments that reward initiative over rigid seniority-gated ownership

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Production-scale data engineering:** Strong at project-scale pipelines (ETL, dashboards); still building depth in large-scale/production data infrastructure. Frame as: eager to apply strong fundamentals in a team that works at greater scale.

## Mapping to Job Posting Language
When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "fast-paced", "ownership", "wear multiple hats", "startup environment", "build from scratch", "0 to 1"

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not deal-breaker):
- "highly structured approval process", "strictly defined scope", "maintenance-only role"

## Management Style Preferences
- Works best with managers who set clear outcomes and then get out of the way
- Less effective under heavy, granular oversight on execution details

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Lead with a concrete build (ShopSense, the GitHub automation at Staytuned) rather than abstract traits
- **CV:** Emphasize measurable, shipped outcomes over responsibilities
- **Interviews:** Use the Staytuned GitHub automation and ShopSense stories to demonstrate ownership and end-to-end execution
- **Don't overstate:** Avoid claiming deep production data-engineering experience at scale; he has strong project-level pipeline experience, not enterprise-scale infrastructure experience yet
